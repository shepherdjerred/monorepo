- Keka 1.6 added a 4 GB FAT32 split option for creating archives that fit on FAT32-formatted drives.
- FAT32 volumes (common on USB drives and older media) cannot hold files larger than 4 GB, so splitting ensures compatibility.
- Choose the FAT32 split size from the split volume options when creating an archive.
